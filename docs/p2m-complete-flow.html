1

Ideation & Validation

Is this product worth building? Market research, competitive analysis, viability scoring, and income math.

P1.1
Product Idea
Define the product, target customer, and unique angle. What problem does it solve? Who buys it?
→ Product concept document
P1.2
Market Research
Search terms, competitor count, price range, review complaints. Amazon and Etsy landscape.
→ Market research summary
P1.3
Competitive Analysis
Top 3-5 competitors, strengths, weaknesses. Where's the gap you fill?
→ Competitive positioning
P1.4
Niche Viability Score
6-factor scoring (min 18/30 to proceed). Demand, competition, margin potential, differentiation, production fit, scalability.
→ Viability score ≥ 18/30
P1.5
Income Target Math
$2k/month backward calculation. Units per day needed, printer capacity, production feasibility.
→ Units/day and printer requirements
▼

⬥ G1 — GO / NO-GO Gate

Score ≥ 18/30 and income math works? → GO to Phase 2. Otherwise archive the idea.

Passes gate → Phase 2: Design & Engineering →

2

Design & Engineering

Turn the validated concept into a real, printable, production-ready product. DIY or contractor path.

Phase 1 Outputs

Market positioning, models needed, price targets, competitive gaps

Workshop Setup

Printer fleet, plate sizes, materials, nozzles, slicer, machine rate

Compliance Check

Material safety, Amazon category rules, consumer regs. Check before designing — may drive choices.

◂

P2.1 — Design Brief / CAD Spec

Complete spec per model. Enough to hand to a contractor or guide your own CAD work.

→ CAD files + spec sheet per model

Multi-Material / Multi-Part

Products may use multiple materials (PETG-HF, PLA, TPU-85A) + purchased hardware (heat set inserts, bolts, nut plates). Spec captures per-part materials and assembly.

▼

Who Designs It?

DIY (OnShape, Fusion, etc.) — or — Contractor (Fiverr, freelancer)

▼
Path A: DIY

You Model in CAD

Export STL/3MF/STEP

Path B: Contractor

Send Brief → Receive Files

Review & revise

▼        ▼

P2.2 — Prototype & Iterate

Print, test, log. Multi-material: test each material + assembly. Repeat until all models pass.

→ Tested prototypes, confirmed dimensions
▸

Define QC Criteria

What's a reject? Warping, adhesion, tolerance, hardware fit, visual defects. Document now.

⟲ FAIL → revise CAD → reprint
▼

Bill of Materials

Printed parts + purchased hardware + packaging components. Feeds Phase 3 COGS directly.

◂

P2.3 — Lock Print Settings + BOM

Finalize slicer profiles. Confirm weights, times, plate capacity. Compile BOM.

→ Locked profiles, production numbers, complete BOM

Planning Notes (for later)

Packaging ideas (→ P4)  |  Photography angles (→ P5)  |  Listing talking points (→ P5)

▼

⬥ G2 — DESIGN LOCK Gate

Design frozen. CAD final. Profiles locked. BOM complete. QC defined. Compliance checked.

Passes gate → Phase 3: Cost Model & Pricing →

3

Cost Model & Pricing

BOM from Phase 2 + Workshop rates = your unit cost. Then build pricing tiers up through distribution and retail.

Phase 2: BOM

Per-model weights, print times, hardware costs, plate capacity

Workshop Ops Plan

Machine hourly rate, material cost/gram, maintenance, labor

P3.1
Per-Unit COGS
Material, machine time, labor, packaging, QC — driven by BOM. Four-Pillar Cost Model.
→ COGS calculation per model
P3.2
Pricing Strategy
Your Cost → Distributor (40% margin) → Wholesale (40%) → Retail (50% keystone). Amazon separate channel.
→ Final selling prices
P3.3
Variation Pricing
Price each model/size/material variant. Multi-pack discounts (3-pk, 5-pk, 12-pk). GITD premium.
→ Pricing per variation
P3.4
Break-Even Analysis
How many units to recover startup costs? Timeline to profitability.
→ Break-even timeline

Pricing locked → Phase 4: Packaging & FBA Prep →

4

Packaging & FBA Prep

Product is designed, costs are known. Now make it shippable — packaging, compliance, batch workflow.

Channel: Amazon Etsy Shopify + Add
Part of the Parallel Sprint Zone — P4 / P5 / P6
P4.1
Packaging Design
Box, insert, labels, poly bag, FNSKU, header card. Product in hand drives packaging decisions.
→ Packaging prototype
Unlocks → P5.3 Photography
P4.2
FBA Prep Checklist
Channel-specific requirements, size tier, weight tier, labels, suffocation warnings, ASIN assignment.
→ FBA compliance
Channel-specific
P4.3
Prep & Ship Workflow
8-step batch process: Print → QC → Pack → Label → Box → Weigh → Ship → Confirm.
→ Batch SOP
P4.1 (Packaging) is a universal blocker — P5.3 Photography cannot start until packaging is complete. P4.2 is channel-specific; Amazon FBA prep differs from Etsy/Shopify fulfillment prep.

Packaging done → Phase 5: Listings & Launch →  (P5.2 keyword research can start in parallel)

5

Listings & Launch

Get it listed, get it live. Barcodes, content, photography, variations, go-live sequence.

Channel: Amazon Etsy Shopify + Add

Title, description, features, dimensions, weight, images, COGS, pricing — shared across all channels. Write once, adapt per channel.

Part of the Parallel Sprint Zone — P4 / P5 / P6

→ Barcode assignment

→ Listing content
Channel-specific format
P5.3
Product Photography
7+ images: main white-bg, lifestyle, features, scale, infographics. Shared across channels.
→ 7 product images
Requires P4.1 Packaging
P5.4
Variations Setup
Parent/child listings for models, colors, packs. Structure varies by channel.
→ Variations configured
Channel-specific
P5.5
Launch Checklist
Per-channel go-live: inventory shipped, listing active, PPC ready, honeymoon strategy.
→ Go-live checklist
Requires P5.2 + P5.3 + P4.2
P5.1 (Barcodes) and P5.2 (Keyword research) can start in parallel with P4 — no dependency on packaging
